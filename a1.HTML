The nepali food momo must try


Momo are steamed dumplings, a staple food in Nepali cuisine. They are made with a dough of flour and water, and filled with a variety of ingredients like meat, vegetables, garlic, and spices. Momos can be served steamed, fried, or in soup form (Jhol Momo). They are often enjoyed with a dipping sauce.Momo is a dumpling made of all-purpose flour and filled with either meat or vegetables. Inspired by Tibetan dumplings, the dish is a very popular Nepali street food. In Nepal the most common type of momos are buff (buffalo) momos followed by chicken.Momo is available in many types like veg for vegeterian, chicken or buff momo for non vegeterian.Momos[a] are a type of steamed filled dumpling in Tibetan[1] and Nepali cuisine[2] that is also popular in neighbouring Bhutan, Bangladesh, and India.[3][4] The majority of Tibetan momos are half-moon in shape like jiaozi, while Nepali momos are normally round like baozi.[5][6] Momos are usually served with a sauce known as achar influenced by the spices and herbs used within many South Asian cuisines.
